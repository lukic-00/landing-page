How do synths work?
Oscillators are the primary sound sources in a synthesizer. They generate basic waveforms such as sine, square, triangle, and sawtooth waves.
Filters shape the timbre of the sound by attenuating or emphasizing certain frequency components.
Amplifiers control the volume or intensity of the sound generated by the oscillators.
Modulation adds movement and variation to the sound by altering certain parameters dynamically over time.
